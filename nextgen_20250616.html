Turn Your Eyes Upon Jesus - Reawaken Hymns



Turn Your Eyes Upon Jesus is written by the blind Helen H. Lemmel in 1918. She authored both the music and lyrics entirely in 1918, despite being blind at the time. The British musician moved to the US when twelve then studied music in Germany in 1907, where she met her husband and returned to US in 1911. A few years after her marriage, a tragic illness caused her to lose her vision. Her husband, refusing to attend to a blind wife, left her. This time of hurt and loss weighed heavily upon Helen.

in 1918, Lemmel was introduced to a pamphlet written by Algerian missionary Lilias Trotter. Trotter was a well-known artist who gave up a lucrative career to serve the Lord on the mission field. Her words stirred the heart of Lemmel. She writes, “Suddenly, as if commanded to stop and listen, I stood still, and, singing in my soul and spirit was the chorus, with not one conscious moment of putting word to word to make rhyme, or note to note to make melody…These verses were written…the same week…” (Micah Hendry in Hymn History: Turn Your Eyes Upon Jesus)

Bible study: Broken but Redeemed

📖 Scriptures:Romans 5:6–8 (NKJV)

“For when we were still without strength, in due time Christ died for the ungodly. For scarcely for a righteous man will one die; yet perhaps for a good man someone would even dare to die. But God demonstrates His own love toward us, in that while we were still sinners, Christ died for us.”

Bible study:

  • A. We Are All Broken
    • We don’t see ourselves fully broken, but we recognize brokenness around us—in sin, pain, and suffering.
    • Once broken, impossible to restore ourselves—only God can mend us.
  • B. Unmerited Gift of Redemption
    • Christ died for us, while still sinners—not after we cleaned up.
    • The focus shifts from works to God’s sacrificial grace, as Paul emphasizes.
  • C. Redemption Turns Brokenness Beautiful
    • Stories of Sarah, Naomi, Moses illustrate how God restores & thrives through brokenness.
    • It’s not OR redemption—it’s redemption through brokenness.

Discussion

  • How do you see brokenness in your own life—even when others don’t?
  • What challenge keeps you from fully believing Christ died for you?
  • How can we welcome redemption through our brokenness, not just after?
  • How does knowing we were undeserving shape our gratitude?

🎬 Movie discussion: Chosen Season 1: Episode 1

Overview: - “I Have Called You By Name”

  • Mary Magdalene (Lilith)
    • Mary is depicted as a woman tormented by demonic possession, living under the name “Lilith.” Despite attempts by religious leaders to help her, she remains in despair until a transformative encounter with Jesus, who calls her by her true name, leading to her healing and redemption.
  • Simon Peter and Andrew
    • The episode portrays Simon and his brother Andrew as fishermen struggling with debt and considering compromising their values to appease Roman authorities. Their desperation highlights the societal pressures faced by many during that time.

🎬 Movie discussion: Chosen Season 1: Episode 1

Overview: - “I Have Called You By Name”

  • Nicodemus
    • A respected Pharisee, Nicodemus is shown grappling with the limitations of his religious understanding when he fails to exorcise demons from Mary. His journey reflects a spiritual awakening and a quest for deeper truth.
  • Matthew
    • Matthew is introduced as a meticulous tax collector, ostracized by his community for collaborating with the Romans. His character sets the stage for exploring themes of acceptance and transformation.
