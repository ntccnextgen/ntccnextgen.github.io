Note

Critical Safety Requirements

  • Minimum 2 adults supervising at ALL times
  • Gender-appropriate supervision required
  • Consider same-gender lock-ins for increased safety

Mandatory Kindness Rules

  1. Kindness is mandatory at all times - no put-downs, bullying, or exclusion
  2. Stay with the group - leaders must always know where you are
  3. No leaving the building - will result in immediate parent call and pickup
  4. Boys and girls never pair off alone - gender boundaries respected
  5. Respect quiet zones after 2am - those sleeping should not be disturbed
  6. No inappropriate language or content - music, videos, conversation
  7. Phone policy: May take photos, no social media scrolling, no contacting people outside event

Adult Supervision Guidelines

  • Designated zones: Assign adults to specific areas
  • Periodic roll calls: Count students every 60-90 minutes
  • Restroom monitoring: Buddy system for students, track who leaves group
  • Lights on during movies: Critical safety requirement - prevents misconduct
  • No blanket sharing: Each student has own blanket
  • Lock remote areas: Restrict access to isolated spaces
  • Monitor exterior doors: Prevent unauthorized departures

Emergency Preparedness

  • Emergency contact list for all students (cell and home numbers)
  • Medical release forms on file
  • First aid kit accessible
  • Designated person to make medical decisions if parents unreachable
  • Know location of nearest hospital
  • Fire exits clearly marked and reviewed with students
  • Church leaders (elder, deacon, executive committee members) informed of event

Food Safety

  • Ask about allergies and dietary restrictions on permission forms
  • Clearly label all food items
  • Provide gluten-free and dairy-free options
  • Proper food handling and temperature control
  • Hand washing before meals
  • Avoid food games that could cause illness

Reporting and Documentation

  • Encourage students to report any inappropriate behavior immediately
  • Document any incidents (injuries, conflicts, rule violations)
  • Contact parents immediately for any safety concerns
  • Keep attendance records throughout night
  • Take photos for documentation (with parental permission)
Important

When to Cancel or Discontinue

If your church cannot implement these safety measures effectively, the lock-in should be cancelled. Student safety is always the top priority. It’s better to postpone or choose a different event than to proceed without adequate safeguards.

Post-Event Follow-Up

  • Debrief with adult team - what went well, what needs improvement
  • Follow up with students during next youth group meeting
  • Document lessons learned for next year
  • Challenge students to continue serving throughout the week
Game 1: Kindness Scavenger Hunt
Tip

Scripture “Let us not become weary in doing good.” - Galatians 6:9

Materials: Scavenger hunt lists, pens, phone cameras

How to Play:
  1. Each member adopt a space or room to clean up or organize
  2. Take photos as proof of completion
  3. Points for creativity and genuine kindness, not just speed
  4. Share favorite moments with whole group afterward
Game 2: Hide and Seek in the Dark (with a Twist)
Tip

Scripture “The greatest among you will be your servant.” - Matthew 23:11

Materials: Flashlights, glow sticks, pre-cleared safe playing area

How to Play:
  1. Glow sticks for seekers, flashlights for hiders, no phones
  2. Traditional hide and seek rules apply
  3. Servant Twist: When found, you must help the seeker find others (joining their team)
  4. Demonstrates: when Jesus finds us, we join His mission to find others
  5. Adult monitors stationed at key locations for safety
  6. Clear boundaries established before game begins
  7. Play multiple rounds with different seekers
Game 3: Love Your Neighbor Spoons
Tip

Scripture “Love your neighbor as yourself.” - Mark 12:31

Materials: Deck of cards, spoons (one fewer than players)

How to Play:
  1. Classic “Spoons” game with kindness rules
  2. When grabbing spoon, must say something kind about person next to you
  3. No aggressive grabbing - gentle and considerate
  4. Player out each round gets to share one way they served someone this week
  5. Emphasizes being aware of others while pursuing goals
Game 4: Footwashing Musical Chairs
Tip

**Scripture “Now that I, your Lord and Teacher, have washed your feet, you also should wash one another’s feet.” - John 13:14

Materials: Chairs, upbeat worship music, wet wipes or towels

How to Play:
  1. Set up chairs in circle (one fewer than players)
  2. Play music while students walk around chairs
  3. When music stops, everyone finds seat
  4. Servant Twist: Person without chair doesn’t “lose” - instead, they choose someone to serve by wiping their shoes or feet with wipe/towel
  5. That person then sits out, and game continues
  6. Everyone gets to serve and be served
Game 5: Sardines (Reverse Hide and Seek)
Tip

Scripture “Where two or three gather in my name, there am I with them.” - Matthew 18:20

Materials: Safe, pre-cleared playing area

How to Play:
  1. One person hides while everyone else seeks
  2. When you find the hider, you quietly join them in hiding spot
  3. Eventually everyone squeezed into one spot like sardines
  4. Last person to find the group hides next round
  5. Teaches: We’re better together, drawing others into community
  6. Keep adequate lighting and safety monitoring
Game 6: Encouragement Dodgeball
Tip

**Scripture “Therefore encourage one another and build each other up.” - 1 Thessalonians 5:11

Materials: Soft foam balls or yarn balls

How to Play:
  1. Traditional dodgeball rules apply
  2. Kindness Rule: When you get someone out, you must give them a genuine compliment
  3. If you get hit, the person who hit you helps you up and high-fives you
  4. Players can get back in by encouraging teammates from sideline
  5. Focus on good sportsmanship and affirming others
Roll the Dice, Break the Ice Questions

Students take turns rolling dice (1-18 using three six-sided dice or numbered cards). Answer the corresponding question, then pass to next person. Leaders participate too!

Toilet Paper Introduction

Setup: Pass around a roll of toilet paper. Tell students to take as much as they want (don’t tell them what it’s for yet!).

The Reveal: For each square they took, they must share one fact about themselves.

Variation - Focus facts on these themes:
  • Things you’re grateful for
  • Ways you like to serve others
  • Your dreams and goals
  • Fun facts about yourself
Leaders go first to model vulnerability and appropriate sharing!
