Mandatory Kindness: Serve Like Jesus

NTCC Youth Lock-In Program Guide

Published

November 20, 2025

Theme Verse

“For even the Son of Man did not come to be served, but to serve, and to give his life as a ransom for many.” - Mark 10:45

Event Overview

Purpose

This lock-in creates a fun, Christ-centered environment where students learn servant leadership through games, fellowship, and intentional kindness. Every activity reinforces our theme: Serving like Jesus means putting others first, showing love in action, and building community.

Key Information

Event Duration: 11/21/2025 Friday 6:00 PM - 11/22/2025 Saturday 9:00 AM

Target Group: 6th grade to college

Required Documents:

  • Signed parental permission and Code of conduct agreement
  • Medical release form
  • Emergency contact information

Complete Lock-In Schedule

6:00 PM - 6:30 PM: Check-In & UNO Join-Up

Activities:

Safety & Precautions

Critical Safety Requirements
  • Minimum 2 adults supervising at ALL times
  • Gender-appropriate supervision required
  • Consider same-gender lock-ins for increased safety

Mandatory Kindness Rules

  1. Kindness is mandatory at all times - no put-downs, bullying, or exclusion
  2. Stay with the group - leaders must always know where you are
  3. No leaving the building - will result in immediate parent call and pickup
  4. Boys and girls never pair off alone - gender boundaries respected
  5. Respect quiet zones after 2am - those sleeping should not be disturbed
  6. No inappropriate language or content - music, videos, conversation
  7. Phone policy: May take photos, no social media scrolling, no contacting people outside event

Adult Supervision Guidelines

  • Designated zones: Assign adults to specific areas
  • Periodic roll calls: Count students every 60-90 minutes
  • Restroom monitoring: Buddy system for students, track who leaves group
  • Lights on during movies: Critical safety requirement - prevents misconduct
  • No blanket sharing: Each student has own blanket
  • Lock remote areas: Restrict access to isolated spaces
  • Monitor exterior doors: Prevent unauthorized departures

Emergency Preparedness

  • Emergency contact list for all students (cell and home numbers)
  • Medical release forms on file
  • First aid kit accessible
  • Designated person to make medical decisions if parents unreachable
  • Know location of nearest hospital
  • Fire exits clearly marked and reviewed with students
  • Church leaders (elder, deacon, executive committee members) informed of event

Food Safety

  • Ask about allergies and dietary restrictions on permission forms
  • Clearly label all food items
  • Provide gluten-free and dairy-free options
  • Proper food handling and temperature control
  • Hand washing before meals
  • Avoid food games that could cause illness

Reporting and Documentation

  • Encourage students to report any inappropriate behavior immediately
  • Document any incidents (injuries, conflicts, rule violations)
  • Contact parents immediately for any safety concerns
  • Keep attendance records throughout night
  • Take photos for documentation (with parental permission)
When to Cancel or Discontinue

If your church cannot implement these safety measures effectively, the lock-in should be cancelled. Student safety is always the top priority. It’s better to postpone or choose a different event than to proceed without adequate safeguards.

Post-Event Follow-Up

  • Debrief with adult team - what went well, what needs improvement
  • Follow up with students during next youth group meeting
  • Document lessons learned for next year
  • Challenge students to continue serving throughout the week
