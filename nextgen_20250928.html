Bible study: Authentic Relationships: Encourage One Another

Theme Scripture for today: Encourage One Another
1 Thessalonians 5:11


“So encourage each other and build each other up, just as you are already doing.” (NLT)

  • Encouragement is more than compliments.
  • Encouragement strengthens the weak.
  • Encouragement builds up the whole body.

Bible study: Encourage One Another (continued)

  1. Encouragement is more than compliments.

  • Compliments usually focus on surface things: “Nice shoes,” “Good game,” “You look great today.”

  • But biblical encouragement goes deeper. It points people back to God’s truth, promises, and hope.

  • It’s not just about making someone “feel good,” but helping them stand firm in their faith.

  • Example: Paul didn’t just say “You’re awesome, church!” He reminded believers of God’s faithfulness, their identity in Christ, and their eternal hope.

  • Illustration: Compliments are like a sugar rush—they give a quick boost but fade fast. Encouragement from God’s Word is like a balanced meal—it gives lasting strength.

Bible study: Encourage One Another (continued)

  1. Encouragement strengthens the weak.

  • Proverbs 12:25: “Anxiety weighs down the heart, but a kind word cheers it up.”

  • Proverbs 25:11 “Timely advice is lovely, like golden apples in a silver basket.”

  • Life is full of pressures: school stress, family struggles, peer pressure. Encouragement helps lift those heavy loads.

  • Even small words can bring life-changing impact.

  • Illustration: Imagine carrying a heavy backpack full of rocks labeled “fear,” “stress,” “failure.” Encouragement is like a friend taking out those rocks one by one.

Bible study: Encourage One Another (continued)

  1. Encouragement builds up the whole body.
  • Paul uses the image of a body (1 Corinthians 12). Each part needs the others to function well.
  • Encouragement is like mortar between bricks—it holds the wall together.
  • When one person is lifted up, the entire community grows stronger.
  • Illustration: Olympic runner Derek Redmond tore his hamstring in the 1992 Barcelona games. As he limped toward the finish line, his father ran onto the track, put his arm around him, and helped him cross. The crowd cheered—not because Derek won, but because his father’s encouragement carried him to the end.

  2. Someone is struggling. How would you think of a creative way to encourage him or her?

Bible study: Authentic Relationships: Love One Another

Theme Scripture for today: Love One Another
Galatians 5:13


“You, my brothers and sisters, were called to be free. But do not use your freedom to indulge the flesh; rather, serve one another humbly in love. (NIV)

  • Freedom leads to service, not selfishness.
  • Service is humble love in action.
  • Service reflects Christ’s character.

Bible study: Love One Another (continued)

  1. Freedom leads to service, not selfishness.

  • Paul reminds believers that Christian freedom is not a license for self-indulgence.

  • Freedom in Christ means we are no longer slaves to sin, so we can freely love and serve others.

  • Real freedom is found in surrender to God, not in doing “whatever I want.”

  • Illustration: Imagine being set free from prison. If someone uses that freedom to commit more crimes, they go right back into chains. But if they use their freedom to build a new life helping others, that freedom has real meaning.

Bible study: Love One Another (continued)

  1. Service is humble love in action.
  • Jesus modeled this when He washed His disciples’ feet (John 13:1–17).
  • Foot washing was the lowest servant’s job, yet Jesus took the towel and knelt down.
  • True service means choosing humility — no task is too small when done in love.

Bible study: Love One Another (continued)

  1. Service reflects Christ’s character.

  • In Matthew 25:40, Jesus says, “Whatever you did for one of the least of these brothers and sisters of mine, you did for me.”

  • Serving others is not just about kindness — it’s worship, because we serve Jesus Himself.

  • When we serve, we reflect Jesus’ heart to the world.

  • Illustration: A mirror doesn’t shine on its own — it reflects light. Our acts of service reflect the light of Christ into the lives of others.

  • Real-Life Story: Mother Teresa served the poorest in Calcutta for decades. She often said, “We are not called to be successful, but faithful.” Her life of humble service became a powerful reflection of Jesus’ love, inspiring millions worldwide.

Bible study: Love One Another: Discussion

Discussion Questions

  1. Why do you think Paul links freedom and service together?
  2. How is serving others an expression of love?
  3. What practical way can we serve others or the church as a youth group?

🎵 Lyric highlights

  • “Change my heart o God” is written by Eddie Espinosa in 1982, this worship classic emerged not in a studio, but from a moment of heartfelt prayer.
  • The inspiration struck while Espinosa was stuck in traffic near Disneyland, praying for God to transform his heart. He began to sing the lyrics spontaneously, as if receiving divine dictation. At the next light, he grabbed scrap paper to jot down the words, preserving the song that would soon resonate worldwide.
  • The lyrics “Change my heart, O God… may I be like You” express raw humility and sincere surrender. It’s a proactive, personal prayer to become more like Jesus.
    • Teaching the story behind the song—written in a traffic jam—helps youth see that God inspires worship even in everyday moments.

Great Is Thy Faithfulness - Reawaken Hymns

1 Great is thy faithfulness, O God my Father; there is no shadow of turning with thee; thou changest not, thy compassions, they fail not; as thou hast been thou forever wilt be.

Refrain: Great is thy faithfulness! Great is thy faithfulness! Morning by morning new mercies I see: all I have needed thy hand hath provided– Great is thy faithfulness, Lord, unto me!


2 Summer and winter and springtime and harvest, sun, moon, and stars in their courses above join with all nature in manifold witness to thy great faithfulness, mercy, and love.

Refrain: Great is thy faithfulness! Great is thy faithfulness! Morning by morning new mercies I see: all I have needed thy hand hath provided– Great is thy faithfulness, Lord, unto me!


3 Pardon for sin and a peace that endureth, thine own dear presence to cheer and to guide, strength for today and bright hope for tomorrow, blessings all mine, with ten thousand beside!

Refrain: Great is thy faithfulness! Great is thy faithfulness! Morning by morning new mercies I see: all I have needed thy hand hath provided– Great is thy faithfulness, Lord, unto me!

🎵 Lyric highlights

  • God’s Unchanging Nature: The hymn emphasizes that God “changest not” and has “no shadow of turning”

  • Daily Mercies: The famous refrain declares “Morning by morning new mercies I see”

  • God’s Provision: Celebrates how “All I have needed thy hand hath provided”

  • Creation’s Witness: References the seasons and natural world as testimony to God’s faithfulness

  • Eternal Promises: Highlights “Pardon for sin and a peace that endureth” and “Strength for today and bright hope for tomorrow”

  • Author Thomas Chisholm wrote this hymp in 1923, which was inspired by simple daily experiences of God’s faithfulness rather than dramatic life events.

I Surrender All - Reawaken Hymns

1 All to Jesus I surrender, All to Him I freely give; I will ever love and trust Him, In His presence daily live.


Refrain: I surrender all, I surrender all; All to Thee, my blessed Savior, I surrender all.


2 All to Jesus I surrender, Make me, Savior, wholly Thine; Let me feel Thy Holy Spirit, Truly know that Thou art mine.


Refrain: I surrender all, I surrender all; All to Thee, my blessed Savior, I surrender all.


3 All to Jesus I surrender, Lord, I give myself to Thee; Fill me with Thy love and power, Let Thy blessing fall on me.


Refrain: I surrender all, I surrender all; All to Thee, my blessed Savior, I surrender all.




🎵 Lyric highlights

Key Themes:

  • Complete Surrender: The central message is yielding “all to Jesus” in every aspect of life
  • Humility and Submission: Emphasizes bowing “humbly at His feet” in reverence
  • Spiritual Transformation: Expresses the desire to be filled with the Holy Spirit
  • Trust and Faith: Represents a deliberate choice made with love and trust
  • Divine Blessing: Includes the prayer “Let thy blessings fall on me”
  • Core Message: This hymn represents the profound spiritual journey of complete surrender to Christ, leading to joy, salvation, and spiritual fulfillment
